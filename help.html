Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What do I need to do for regular user maintenance?

A: Whenever your watch is exposed to sea water or mud, rinse it off with clean fresh water.

Q: How do I replace batteries?

A: Leave battery replacement up to your original retailer or an authorized service center. Have the batteries replaced only with the type specified in the User's Guide. Use of a different battery type can cause malfunction.

Q: What should I do if the inside of the glass becomes fogged?

A:The inside surface of the watch glass may fog when the watch is exposed to a sudden drop in temperature. No problem is indicated if the fogging clears up relatively quickly.

Q: Do you have a retail store I can visit?

A: Our main retail store is located at TARUMT, go to the About page to learn more.

Q: How do I set the time on my watch?

Most watches have a crown that can be pulled out to different positions for time setting. You pull the crown out, rotate it to adjust the hour, minute, and sometimes the date. Always set the time in the correct direction to avoid damaging the movement.

Q: Can I wear my watch while swimming or diving?

It depends on the water resistance rating of your watch. Watches with a higher rating (e.g., 200m or more) can typically be worn while swimming or diving. Always check the manufacturer's specifications before exposing your watch to water.

Q: How long does a watch battery last?

A typical watch battery lasts between 1-3 years, depending on the type of watch and its features. For instance, watches with additional functions like alarms or backlighting may consume more power.

Q: How often does a watch need to be serviced?

Like any high-precision instrument, a watch needs to be serviced regularly to ensure optimum functioning. The servicing frequency depends on the model, the climate and the care you take of it. As a general rule, we recommend that you have a service carried out every four to five years by a service centre.

Q: How do I choose the right watch for me?

Consider your style (formal, casual, sporty), functionality (chronograph, smartwatch, water resistance), and features (battery life, fitness tracking, etc.). Also, think about your budget and preferences for brand, design, and materials.

Q: How do I adjust my watch bracelet or band?

Many watch bracelets have removable links or clasps that can be adjusted. Some stores or watch repair services can also assist in resizing if needed.
